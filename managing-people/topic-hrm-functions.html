HRM Functions: Recruitment, Selection, Training, Appraisal

In Simple Language

Human Resource Management (HRM) is the strategic approach to managing the employee life-cycle. Core functions: HR Planning (forecasting demand and supply); Job Analysis (Job Description + Job Specification); Recruitment (attracting candidates — internal sources like promotions, transfers, job posting vs external sources like job portals, campus, agencies); Selection (application, tests — aptitude, personality, psychomotor, integrity — interviews — structured/unstructured/panel/stress, background checks, medical, final offer); Onboarding & Socialisation; Training & Development (on-the-job: rotation, coaching, mentoring; off-the-job: classroom, simulation, case study, e-learning); Performance Appraisal (methods: graphic rating, BARS, MBO, 360-degree, forced ranking; appraisal errors: halo, leniency, central tendency, recency); Compensation & Benefits (base pay, variable pay, benefits, equity); and Employee Relations & Separation (resignation, retirement, layoff, dismissal).

Key Concepts

  • HR Planning: demand and supply forecasting
  • Job Analysis: Job Description (duties) vs Job Specification (KSAOs)
  • Recruitment sources: internal vs external
  • Selection tools: tests, interviews (structured > unstructured for validity)
  • Training methods: on-the-job vs off-the-job
  • Performance Appraisal methods (GRS, BARS, MBO, 360°, Forced Ranking)
  • Appraisal errors: halo, leniency, central tendency, recency, similarity
  • Compensation: base + variable + benefits + equity
  • Separations: voluntary vs involuntary

Short Answers Short-Answer Q&A

Differentiate Job Description and Job Specification.
Job Description outlines the duties, responsibilities, working conditions and reporting relationships of a job. Job Specification lists the knowledge, skills, abilities, qualifications, experience and other personal attributes (KSAOs) needed to perform the job.
Compare internal and external recruitment.
Internal recruitment (promotions, transfers, job posting) is faster, cheaper, motivating and uses known performers but limits fresh ideas. External recruitment (portals, agencies, campus) brings new perspectives and skills but is costlier, riskier and slower.
List three common appraisal errors.
Halo (one trait colours all), Leniency (too generous), Central Tendency (cluster at average), Recency (only recent events recalled), Similarity (favour those like rater), and Personal Bias.
What is 360-degree feedback?
A performance appraisal method that collects feedback from multiple sources — supervisor, peers, subordinates, self and sometimes customers — to give a holistic, balanced view of an employee's performance and behaviour.

Long Answer 150-Word Answer

Human Resource Management (HRM) is the strategic, integrated approach to managing an organization's most valuable asset — its people — across the full employee life-cycle. It begins with Human Resource Planning, in which the organization forecasts its demand for talent (driven by strategy, growth and attrition) and the supply available internally and externally, then plans to close any gap. Job Analysis underpins everything: it produces the Job Description (duties, responsibilities, reporting lines, working conditions) and the Job Specification (the knowledge, skills, abilities and other characteristics — KSAOs — required to perform the job). Recruitment attracts a qualified pool of candidates using internal sources (promotion, transfer, job posting, employee referral) — which are fast, motivating and well-validated — and external sources (campus, job portals, social media, agencies, walk-ins) which inject fresh talent and perspectives. Selection narrows the pool through application screening, tests (aptitude, personality, psychomotor, integrity, work-sample), interviews (structured interviews have substantially higher predictive validity than unstructured), reference and background checks, medical examinations and finally a written offer. Onboarding and socialisation then convert the new hire into a productive, engaged employee. Training and Development upgrade skills via on-the-job methods (job rotation, coaching, mentoring, understudy assignments) and off-the-job methods (classroom training, case studies, simulations, role-play, e-learning, outdoor experiential programmes). Performance Appraisal evaluates results and behaviour; common methods include Graphic Rating Scales, Behaviourally Anchored Rating Scales (BARS), Management by Objectives (MBO), 360-degree feedback, and forced-ranking systems. Raters must guard against errors — halo, leniency, central tendency, recency, similarity and personal bias. Compensation and Benefits design balances base pay, variable incentives, statutory and discretionary benefits, and equity; the structure must be internally fair, externally competitive and aligned with strategy. Finally, separation processes — voluntary resignation, retirement, layoff or dismissal — must be handled professionally, with dignity and in compliance with labour law.
