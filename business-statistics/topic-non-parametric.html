Other Non-Parametric Tests

In Simple Language

Non-parametric tests work without strict distributional assumptions and are ideal for small samples, ordinal data or non-normal continuous data. Sign test — tests whether the median of differences (paired data) is zero, using only the signs of differences. Mann-Whitney U / Wilcoxon rank-sum — non-parametric alternative to the two independent-samples t-test; ranks all observations and compares rank sums. Kruskal-Wallis — non-parametric alternative to one-way ANOVA, comparing several groups. Spearman rank correlation rₛ — assesses monotonic association between two ranked variables. These tests trade some power for distributional robustness.

Key Concepts

  • When to use non-parametric vs parametric tests
  • Sign test for paired data (median of differences = 0)
  • Mann-Whitney U (Wilcoxon rank-sum) for two independent samples
  • Kruskal-Wallis H-test for k independent samples (ranks of all observations)
  • Spearman rank correlation rₛ for monotonic relationship
  • Trade-off: distribution-free but lower power than parametric counterparts

Short Answers Short-Answer Q&A

When should you prefer a non-parametric test?
When sample sizes are small and normality cannot be assumed, when data is ordinal/ranked, or when outliers heavily distort means and variances.
Difference between Mann-Whitney U and t-test.
Mann-Whitney compares distributions of two independent samples using ranks; the t-test compares means assuming normality and equal variance.
State the null hypothesis of the Kruskal-Wallis test.
All k populations have the same distribution (often interpreted as equal medians under similar shapes).
How is Spearman's rₛ computed?
Convert both variables to ranks and compute Pearson correlation on the ranks; values range from −1 to +1.

Long Answer 150-Word Answer

Non-parametric tests are inferential procedures that make minimal assumptions about the population distribution and are therefore the right choice when sample sizes are small, data are ordinal or skewed, or outliers distort parametric statistics. The sign test analyses paired data (or a single sample against a hypothesised median) by examining only the signs of the differences; under H₀ the number of positive signs follows a binomial(n, 0.5). The Mann-Whitney U test (equivalent to the Wilcoxon rank-sum test) compares the distributions of two independent samples by pooling all observations, ranking them and summing the ranks for each group — it is the non-parametric counterpart of the two-sample t-test. The Kruskal-Wallis H-test generalises this to k independent samples and is the non-parametric alternative to one-way ANOVA, with chi-square distribution under H₀. The Spearman rank correlation rₛ measures the strength of a monotonic — not necessarily linear — relationship between two variables, computed as Pearson correlation on the ranks. Because they replace numeric values with ranks, non-parametric tests are robust to outliers and distributional violations, but they sacrifice some statistical power compared to parametric tests when the parametric assumptions actually hold. In practice, smart analysts check assumptions first, prefer parametric tests when justified, and reach for non-parametric tools when assumptions are doubtful or data are inherently ordinal.
