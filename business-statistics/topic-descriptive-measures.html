Numerical Descriptive Measures

In Simple Language

Descriptive measures summarise numerical data in a few numbers. Central tendency: mean (arithmetic average — sensitive to outliers), median (middle value — robust), mode (most frequent). Variation: range, interquartile range (Q3 − Q1), variance, standard deviation, coefficient of variation. Shape: skewness (asymmetry) and kurtosis (peakedness). The five-number summary (Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max) plus a box plot shows centre, spread and outliers at a glance. For populations we use μ and σ; for samples we use x̄ and s (dividing by n−1 to be unbiased).

Key Concepts

  • Mean, median, mode — when each is appropriate
  • Range, IQR, variance, standard deviation, coefficient of variation
  • Skewness (right/left), kurtosis
  • Five-number summary and box plot; outlier rule (1.5×IQR)
  • Empirical rule (68-95-99.7) for bell-shaped data
  • Chebyshev's theorem for any distribution

Quiz Practice Quiz

Q1. Which central-tendency measure is most resistant to outliers?
  • Mean
  • Median
  • Mode
  • Geometric mean
Explanation: The median uses only ranks, so extreme values do not shift it.
Q2. The Empirical Rule says approximately what % of bell-shaped data lies within ±2σ?
  • 68%
  • 75%
  • 95%
  • 99.7%
Explanation: Within ±1σ ≈ 68%, ±2σ ≈ 95%, ±3σ ≈ 99.7%.
Q3. Coefficient of variation is best used to:
  • Detect outliers
  • Compare variability of variables with different units
  • Test hypotheses
  • Compute confidence intervals
Explanation: CV = (s/x̄)×100% is unit-free, allowing comparison across variables.
Q4. For positively (right) skewed data:
  • Mean = Median = Mode
  • Mean < Median < Mode
  • Mode < Median < Mean
  • Median < Mode < Mean
Explanation: A long right tail pulls the mean above the median, which is above the mode.

Short Answers Short-Answer Q&A

Difference between range and IQR.
Range = Max − Min (very sensitive to outliers). IQR = Q3 − Q1 covers the middle 50% and is resistant to outliers.
Why divide by n − 1 for the sample variance?
Bessel's correction makes s² an unbiased estimator of population variance σ² when based on a sample.
State the 1.5×IQR rule for outliers.
Values below Q1 − 1.5×IQR or above Q3 + 1.5×IQR are flagged as potential outliers.
When is the median preferred over the mean?
For skewed distributions or data with outliers (income, house prices), where the mean misrepresents the typical value.

Long Answer 150-Word Answer

Numerical descriptive measures condense an entire dataset into a handful of interpretable numbers and form the basis of every subsequent statistical method. Central tendency answers 'what is typical?' — the mean averages all values and is efficient but sensitive to outliers; the median is the middle observation and is robust; the mode is the most frequent value, useful for categorical data and multimodal distributions. Variation answers 'how spread out are the data?' — range uses only extremes, IQR (Q3 − Q1) captures the middle 50% and resists outliers, while variance and standard deviation use every observation and feed directly into probability theory. The coefficient of variation (s/x̄) standardises spread for cross-variable comparisons. Shape statistics — skewness and kurtosis — describe asymmetry and tail-heaviness; in right-skewed data the mean exceeds the median, in left-skewed it is below. The five-number summary (Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max) visualised as a box plot reveals centre, spread, skewness and outliers (1.5×IQR rule) on one chart. For bell-shaped data the empirical rule (68-95-99.7) ties the mean and standard deviation to coverage probabilities; for any distribution Chebyshev guarantees at least 1 − 1/k² of values lie within k standard deviations. Choosing the right descriptive measure depends on data shape and the business question.
