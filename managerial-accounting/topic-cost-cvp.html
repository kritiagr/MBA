Cost Concepts and Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP) Analysis

In Simple Language

Costs are classified many ways: by behaviour — Fixed (don't change with output, e.g. rent) vs Variable (vary with output, e.g. raw material); by traceability — Direct vs Indirect; by function — Manufacturing (Prime = direct material + direct labour; Conversion = direct labour + factory overhead) vs Period; by decision relevance — Sunk, Opportunity, Differential. CVP analysis examines how profits respond to changes in volume, price and costs. Contribution Margin = Sales − Variable Cost; CM ratio = Contribution / Sales. Break-Even Point (BEP) in units = Fixed Cost / Contribution per unit; in rupees = Fixed Cost / CM ratio. Margin of Safety = (Actual − BEP) / Actual. Sales for target profit = (Fixed Cost + Target Profit) / Contribution per unit.

Key Concepts

  • Cost behaviour: Fixed, Variable, Mixed (Semi-variable)
  • Cost traceability: Direct vs Indirect
  • Prime Cost = DM + DL; Conversion Cost = DL + FOH
  • Sunk cost (irrelevant), Opportunity cost, Differential cost
  • Contribution Margin and CM Ratio
  • Break-Even Point (units and rupees)
  • Margin of Safety; sales required for a target profit
  • CVP assumptions: linearity, constant selling price, constant cost behaviour

Quiz Practice Quiz

Q1. Fixed cost per unit:
  • Stays constant
  • Increases with volume
  • Decreases as volume increases
  • Equals variable cost
Explanation: Total fixed cost is constant, so per-unit fixed cost falls as units rise.
Q2. Contribution per unit = ?
  • Selling Price − Total Cost
  • Selling Price − Variable Cost per unit
  • Selling Price − Fixed Cost per unit
  • Fixed Cost / Units
Explanation: It is the unit margin available to cover fixed costs and provide profit.
Q3. BEP in units =
  • Fixed Cost / Contribution per unit
  • Variable Cost / Contribution
  • Sales / Variable Cost
  • Profit / Contribution
Explanation: At BEP, contribution exactly covers fixed costs; no profit, no loss.
Q4. Margin of Safety =
  • Sales / Fixed Cost
  • (Actual Sales − BE Sales) / Actual Sales
  • Profit / Sales
  • BE Sales / Actual Sales
Explanation: It is the cushion by which sales exceed break-even, often expressed as a percentage.

Short Answers Short-Answer Q&A

Distinguish Fixed and Variable cost.
Fixed cost stays constant in total over a relevant range (e.g. rent); per unit it falls as volume rises. Variable cost varies in total with volume (e.g. raw material); per unit it is constant.
State the BEP formula.
BEP (units) = Fixed Cost / Contribution per unit. BEP (₹) = Fixed Cost / (Contribution per unit / Selling Price) = Fixed Cost / CM Ratio.
Define Margin of Safety.
Margin of Safety = Actual Sales − Break-even Sales (in units or rupees), often expressed as a percentage of actual sales; a higher MOS indicates lower risk of loss.
List two assumptions of CVP analysis.
(1) Selling price, variable cost per unit and total fixed cost remain constant over the relevant range. (2) Costs can be cleanly separated into fixed and variable components. (3) Single product or constant sales mix.

Long Answer 150-Word Answer

Cost concepts and Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP) analysis equip managers to make short-term planning and pricing decisions. Costs are classified along several dimensions. By behaviour they are Fixed (constant in total within a relevant range, like rent or salaries), Variable (varying in total with activity, like raw material and direct labour) or Mixed (containing both elements). By traceability they are Direct (assigned to a single cost object) or Indirect (common to multiple objects and allocated). By function manufacturing costs include Direct Material, Direct Labour and Factory Overhead — combined as Prime Cost (DM + DL) or Conversion Cost (DL + FOH) — while Period costs (SG&A) are expensed when incurred. Decision-relevant categories include Sunk costs (already incurred, never relevant), Opportunity costs (forgone benefit of the next-best alternative) and Differential costs (incremental between options). CVP analysis builds on the cost-behaviour split to ask how profit responds to changes in volume, price and cost. The contribution margin (CM = Sales − Variable Cost) is the amount each rupee of sales contributes toward covering fixed costs and then generating profit. The contribution margin ratio (CM/Sales) captures this on a percentage basis. The break-even point is where contribution exactly equals fixed cost: BEP units = Fixed Cost / Contribution per unit, BEP rupees = Fixed Cost / CM Ratio. Sales required for a target profit follow the same logic: (Fixed Cost + Target Profit) / Contribution per unit. The margin of safety, (Actual − BEP) ÷ Actual, measures the cushion above break-even and is a quick gauge of operating risk. CVP assumes linearity over a relevant range, separable cost behaviour and constant sales mix, but within those boundaries it is one of the most actionable tools in management accounting.
